About Me

Bridging behavior,
health, and technology

I am a doctoral candidate at Florida State University's School of Information. Before the PhD, I was a faculty member in information systems at Institut Teknologi Kalimantan (ITK) — my home institution in East Kalimantan, Indonesia.

My research sits at the crossroads of health informatics and behavioral science. I am particularly interested in how socioeconomic and cultural factors shape health technology adoption in archipelagic nations — spaces where geography makes physical healthcare inaccessible for millions.

Research Focus

Dissertation & Publications

My work examines the socio-behavioral determinants of telehealth uptake in Indonesia — a country of 17,000+ islands where digital health could be transformative, if barriers are properly understood.

Dissertation 2025–2026

Socio-Behavioral Factors in Telehealth Adoption in Indonesia

Applying the Health Belief Model (HBM) to identify how perceived severity, benefits, barriers, cues to action, and self-efficacy shape telehealth adoption among Indonesian adults. Hypotheses H1–H9 tested through survey methodology.
